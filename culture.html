ABOUT / TATTOO CULTURE

ORIGINS OF TATTOOS

The word tattoo originated from the Polynesian word "ta" meaning striking something and the tahitian word "tatau" meaning to mark something.

Tattooing has been practiced for centuries in many culturesv from the Neolithic times and mummies from the end of the 2nd millennium BC. Sailors introduced the word and concept of tattooing to Europe.

TATTOO CULTURE TODAY

Tattoo in the Western world was once viewed as a stigma, but is now becoming more accepted and can even be seen trending among celebrities.

However, in terms of learning the art of tattooing, it is kept secretive amongst tattoo artists. One can not learn the proper technique or find information on equipments used on the Internet. Tattoo artists are extremely conservative (and traditional) in this sense. One can only learn the true professional art through apprenticeship.

Uses

Tattoos have been used for religious and tribal markings, slave and prisoner identification, gang organization, bearing of ranks and accomplishments, medical practices, cosmetics, and aesthetics.
