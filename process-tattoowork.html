INKING THE SKIN

Tattoos work by penetrating the skin and injecting ink into the 2nd layer of the skin. This is done by using an electrically powered tattoo machine to deposit a drop of insoluble ink into the dermis layer of the skin per penetration. The artist then moves the machine to create lines. When you see a tattoo, you're actually seeing the ink through the epidermis, or the outer layer of skin. To avoid getting a tattoo infection, make sure the area being tattooed has been shaved and disinfected.

Tattoo Machines

  • A sterilized needle
  • A tube system, which draws the ink through the machine
  • An electric motor
  • A foot pedal, like those used on sewing machines, which controls the vertical movement of the needle.
