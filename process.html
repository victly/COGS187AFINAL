TATTOO PROCESS / THE STUDIO

HOW TATTOOS WORK


Tattoos work by penetrating the skin and injecting ink into the 2nd layer of the skin. This is done by using an electrically powered tattoo machine to deposit a drop of insoluble ink into the dermis layer of the skin per penetration. The artist then moves the machine to create lines. When you see a tattoo, you're actually seeing the ink through the epidermis, or the outer layer of skin.

FINDING THE RIGHT ARTIST


It is very important that you find an artist that you feel comfortable with. Each artist has their own approach and style, and you want to make sure you find the right one to create a permanent symbol on your body.

To find the right artist:
  • Look at their portfolio to see if it matches with the styles you're looking for
  • Ask questions, such as "What kind of needles do you use?", "How do you prepare when giving a tattoo?", and "Can you describe to me the kind of environment I will be getting my tattoo done?"
  • Talk to the artist to see that you are comfortable with them.
------
A tattoo is a permanent mark on your body. It is vital that you make sure that you are comfortable with the entire process before making the decision.

HEALING & AFTERCARE


After first getting a tattoo, make sure to wait at least 2-24 hours before removing the bandage. It protects your tattoo from airbourne bacteria. Once you remove your tattoo, gently wash the area with lukewarm water and mild, unscented liquid antibacterial soap. Then let it dry, apply ointment, and continue to wash and moisturize it 3-5 times a day.

For long term: avoid soaking the tattoo, exposing it to direct sunlight, scratching or picking at your tattoo, wearing tight-fitting clothing, working out, and swelling (i.e. standing too long causes your feet to swell).
