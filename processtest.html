TATTOO PROCESS / START TO END

Preparing & What to Expect


Know what you're looking for

Checklist of Q's:
  • Look at their portfolio to see if it matches with the styles you're looking for.
  • Ask questions, such as "How long have you been tattooing?", "What about tattooing do you like the most?", and "Can you describe to me the kind of environment I will be getting my tattoo done?"
  • Talk to the artist to see that you are comfortable with them. It is vital that you make sure that you are comfortable with the entire process before making the decision.
HOW TATTOOS WORK


Inking Skin Tattoos work by penetrating the skin and injecting ink into the 2nd layer of the skin. This is done by using an electrically powered tattoo machine to deposit a drop of insoluble ink into the dermis layer of the skin per penetration. The artist then moves the machine to create lines. When you see a tattoo, you're actually seeing the ink through the epidermis, or the outer layer of skin.

Check out the tattooing process slideshow above!

Modern tattoo machines & basic components:
  • A sterilized needle
  • A tube system, which draws the ink through the machine
  • An electric motor
  • A foot pedal, like those used on sewing machines, which controls the vertical movement of the needle.

HEALING & AFTERCARE


After first getting a tattoo, make sure to wait at least 2-24 hours before removing the bandage. It protects your tattoo from airbourne bacteria. Once you remove your tattoo, gently wash the area with lukewarm water and mild, unscented liquid antibacterial soap. Then let it dry, apply ointment, and continue to wash and moisturize it 3-5 times a day.

Some recommended lotions to avoid flaky skin and scabbing of the tattoo are: Lubriderm or Aquaphor lotion. Always go with the most basic, unscented lotion.

For long term: avoid soaking the tattoo, exposing it to direct sunlight, scratching or picking at your tattoo, wearing tight-fitting clothing, working out, and swelling (i.e. standing too long causes your feet to swell).
